Why call rate mater

Average coverage isn’t sufficient

Many labs often describe an assay’s coverage primarily in terms of the mean coverage across the entire assay, but that only tells part of the story.  An assay’s coverage can vary greatly within a genome, exome, or panel. For example, regions may have reduced coverage due to high or low GC content or because other similar sequences reduce the mapping quality of the reads. As a result, a test with an average of 100x coverage may have large regions with no coverage at all even though the assay’s overall average is high.

Call rate helps to identify gaps in coverage

What is important to understand about the performance of an assay is the percentage of the region that receives trustable results (known as the call rate). Looking at base-by-base call rates can reveal coverage gaps, even in assays with high average coverage. We optimize the Exome+® assay for uniform read distribution across regions that are important for specific types of genetic testing. For these genes, we provide high-confidence calls at a rate of ≥99.5%, with base-by-base coverage at >20x coverage.
